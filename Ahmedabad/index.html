Historical Places

Delhi Attractions

Red Fort

UNESCO World Heritage Site and symbol of Mughal grandeur, showcasing Indo‑Islamic architecture.

Qutub Minar

The tallest brick minaret in the world with rich Indo‑Islamic details from Delhi’s medieval era.

Humayun's Tomb

A Mughal masterpiece and precursor to the Taj Mahal featuring elegant Persian influences.

India Gate

A prominent war memorial in the heart of Delhi honoring soldiers of World War I.

Lucknow Attractions

Gandhi Ashram

A serene campus preserving Gandhi’s life, exhibits, and the historic Dandi March legacy.

Adalaj Stepwell

A 15th‑century stepwell with intricate Gujarati‑Maru and Islamic carvings amid calm gardens.

Sidi Saiyyed Mosque

Renowned for its stone lattice windows, especially the famous tree‑of‑life jali.

Kankaria Lake

A vast urban lake with zoo, rides, and evening light shows for family outings.

Choose Your Travel Mode

Flights

Fastest & Most Convenient

Duration ~1.5-2 hours
Frequency Multiple Daily
Best For Business

The fastest way to travel between Delhi and Ahmedabad. Flights are frequent and offered by several airlines, with competitive prices. Early booking recommended for cheaper fares.

Trains

Economical & Comfortable

Duration ~15-18 hours
Options Rajdhani Express
Best For Budget Travel

Trains offer an economical yet comfortable option. The journey takes 15-18 hours depending on the train. Multiple classes available, making them suitable for various budgets.

Road Trip

Scenic & Adventurous

Distance ~930-940 km
Route NH48
Best For Adventure

A road trip via NH48 offers scenic views of lakes, hills, and towns. Perfect for those who love long drives and want to explore historical landmarks and regional cuisines.
